Chapter 5

(THIS CHAPTER CONTAINS NON-ENGLISH PHRASES. PLEASE HAVE YOUR TRANSLATOR READY)

Aiden, on the other hand, was worried.

He put down his phone and sighed

His room was a mess

Homework everywhere

Torn-up paper on the floor

A water spill he said he’d ‘ clean later’

Fur from his tail everywhere (it’s shedding Period.)

The only “neat“ places in his room, were his bed, and his desk.

His bed wasn’t anything special. Just a bed with green accents and a pansexual pride flag hanging on the wall next to it.

He sighed dramatically , slumping forward in his chair as he ran a hand through his hair, messing it up even more than it already was.

“… Why do I feel like he’s lying…?”

He thought to himself as he got up and stretched a bit

‘Should I press further…? I don’t want to make him uncomfortable though….’

His thoughts were quickly interrupted by a knock on his door

“Roberto, querido, es hora de cenar”

He turned. It was his mom. She’s the only one who calls him by his middle name.

“Está bien mamá, bajaré en in minuto” he said, with a smile

She smiled back. Then left.

By midnight, Aiden was wide awake, watching random Building videos on his phone

His mind drifted to Marin

He sighed

‘ Why do I even care so much…?’ he wondered to himself.

His tail curled around his thigh

He was emotionally invested

He couldn’t back down now

Not yet

not now.

‘ I need to know him better. I don’t know why, I just do. I’ll try to learn as much as I can about him… If my ADHD allows me to focus, that is…’

So he just sighed

And sent Marin a text

Not a long, heavy text, no

Just a meme

He waited till Marin and saw the message

And smiled when he saw Marin reply with a laughing emoji.

The next day at school…

Class seemed to drag on forever

Marin, who usually loved learning, found himself unable to focus

“You’re not alone…”

The words were still stuck in his head as he stared at the open notebook in front of him.

His handwriting, usually neat, tidy and impeccable, was now slightly lopsided, and the book had small, unintelligible doodles in the margins

By the time it was lunch break, Marian realized he did bring money, but he gave it to a junior, who didn’t have any

As he was headed towards the same secluded part of the school, he bumped into someone. A girl.

“O-oh! I’m sorry, are you OK?” He asked, dusting off his shirt and looking at her.

“Next time watch where you’re-“ she started, but stop in the middle of her sentence when she got a good look at his face

Marin also got a good look at her

His panic sparked a bit. Because he had him bumped into just some random girl, he bumped into the principal’s daughter. A devil, for that matter.

Devils, were a higher ranked demonic species which look almost identical to humans, if not for their fangs

“S-sorry, I’m really sorry, I didn’t mean to –“ he started

“Stop apologizing. I’m not dead, am I?“ She said, cocking, her head to the side with a hand on her hip as she looked him over once again

“What’s your name? She said abruptly.

Marin Flinched at the directness, then said “ oh, oh… My name’s Marin…”

“Marin…” she repeated, testing the name on her tongue. “Cool name. I’m Maddie”

“It’s nice to… Meet you…”

“Same here”

“Uh… I … have to go… sorry…“

Marin muttered Under his breath, before hurriedly walking away

Maddie stared after him, lips curling up into a smirk.

As Marin approached that secluded spot once again, he noticed that Aiden was already there.

Waiting.

Aiden held a lighter, but no cigarette this time.

Just leaning against the wall, lighter in hand, and the smell of fresh food wafting in the cold air

For a second, Marin considered walking away

But, he felt his feet, walking forward

Aiden’s ears perked up as he heard Marin approach

“What are you doing here…?” Marin asked, crossing his arms defensively as he looked away. “You’re popular, don’t you have a clique to be with…?”

“Well, I came here to see you. Where’s your lunch? Or your lunch money“ Aiden said, pushing off the wall, putting the now closed lighter in his pocket as he walked over to Marin .

“… I gave my money to a junior” Marin said quietly “… Why’d you wanna know?”

“Well, I packed too much lunch today. I figured I’d share with you if you didn’t have any” Aiden said, offering a sandwich.

‘Oh, so THAT'S where the scent was coming from…’ Marin thought to himself.

Marin’s hands shook a bit

He wanted to decline

But his hands took the sandwich

and his mouth opened, letting out a quiet “thank you”

It was warm

His mouth watered

“You’re welcome” Aiden replied with a smile

Marin slowly unwrapped the plastic wrap from the sandwich before taking a bite

Color warmed his cheeks

A small noise of pleasure escaped his lips

“… This is really good…” Marin said, mouth full

“I’m glad you like it” Aiden said

When Marin finished the sandwich, he felt like licking his fingers

That sandwich was the second thing he’d eaten since the whole day. The first being a red apple.

“You have crumbs on your face” Aiden said, reaching out a hand as though to wipe it off, but stopped himself “should I help you get it off…?”

Marin hesitated a bit. Before he nodded slowly.

He was starting to trust Aiden

It felt absolutely terrifying

But also…nice? Warm?

He didn’t know what to think.

Aiden felt relieved.

He thought he’d make Marin uncomfortable, that’s why he hesitated.

But seeing Marin’s slow nod, signaling he had permission to proceed, made him nearly let out a breath he didn’t know he’d been holding.

He tentatively lifted a hand to Marin’s face, using his thumb to wipe crumbs off the corner of Marin’s lips

Their eyes met.

Aiden’s touch lingered for just a second longer than necessary before pulling away.

“There. It’s gone now.” Aiden said, their gazes still locked together

“Thanks…” Marin said, shyly looking away as he tucked a stray hair strand behind his ear.

'I feel weird...' Marin thought to himself as he felt flutters throughout his entire body
